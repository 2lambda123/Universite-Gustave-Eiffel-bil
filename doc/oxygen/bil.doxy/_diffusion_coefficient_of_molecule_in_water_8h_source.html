24 /*
25  * Useful methods/equations to calculate diffusion coefficients
26  *
27  * 1- William A. Tucker and Leslie H. Nelken:
28  *
29  * D_water = 13.26e-5 / (h^{1.14} (VB')^{0.589})
30  *
31  * h is the viscosity of water
32  * VB' is the LaBas estimate of molar volume
33  *
34  * These estimation methods were developed by Diffusion Coefficients in Air and Water by William A. Tucker and Leslie H. Nelken, Chapter 17 in Handbook of Chemical Property Estimation Methods, Warren J. Lyman, William F. Reehl and David H. Rosenblatt, editors, American Chemical Society, 1982.
35  *
36  * 2- Stokes-Einstein Relation (for large molecules):
37  *
38  * D = kT/(6 p h r)
39  *
40  * k Boltzman constant (1.38e-23 J/K)
41  * h is the viscosity of water
42  * r is the radius of sphere (molecule)
43  *
44  * 3- Sutherland-Einstein Correlation (for smaller molecules):
45  * Modification of Stokes-Einstein which considers sliding friction
46  * between solute and solvent.
47  *
48  * D = kT/(6 p h r) * (b r + 3h)/(b r + 2h)
49  *
50  * 4- Wilke-Chang Correlation (for small molecules):
51  * Semi-empirical modification of the Stokes-Einstein relation
52  * 10-15% error.
53  * Wike C.R., Chang P., Correlation of diffusion coefficients in diulte solutions, A.1.Ch.E. Journal, p 264-270, 1955.
54  *
55  * D = 7.4e-8 (xM)^0.5 T / (h V^0.6)
56  *
57  * where x = 2.6 (for water)
58  * M = Molecular weight of solvent (18 g/mole for water)
59  * V = Molal volume of solute (m3/kg)
60  */
