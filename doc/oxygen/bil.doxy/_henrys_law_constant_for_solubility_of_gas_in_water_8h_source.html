9 /*
10  * Henry's law constant is defined usually by
11  *
12  * k_H = c_a / p_g
13  *
14  * c_a = concentration of the species in the aqueous phase
15  * p_g = partial pressure of that species in the gas phase
16  * k_H refers to standard conditions T0 = 298.15
17  *
18  * Henry's law constant can also be expressed as the dimensionless ratio
19  * between the aqueous phase concentration c_a of a species and its gas
20  * phase concentration c_g:
21  *
22  * kc_H = c_a / c_g = k_H * RT
23  *
24  * A simple way to describe Henry's law as a function of temperature is
25  *
26  * k_H = k_H0 * exp( A * (1/T - 1/T0) )
27  *
28  * where A = - DH/R with DH the enthalpy of solution.
29  *
30  * Below the k_H definition is used.
31  *
32  * The official SI unit is mol/m3/Pa. The common used unit is mol/L/bar.
33  * Conversions:
34  * 1 mol/(m3*Pa) = 101.325 mol/(L*bar)
35  * 1 mol/(L*bar) = 9.87e-3 mol/(m3*Pa)
36  */
